The Donation Process

1

Donate Food

Donate non-perishable food items at our collection points or schedule a pickup for larger donations. We accept canned goods, grains, and other shelf-stable items.

2

Collection & Sorting

Our volunteers collect and sort all donations, checking expiration dates and packaging integrity to ensure food safety standards are met.

3

Distribution

Sorted food is distributed to local food banks, shelters, and community centers where it reaches individuals and families in need.

4

Monetary Donations

Financial contributions help us purchase fresh produce, dairy, and other perishable items that complement non-perishable donations.

5

Volunteer Support

Volunteers are the backbone of our operation, helping with collection, sorting, distribution, and administrative tasks.

6

Community Impact

Your donations directly impact local families, providing nutritious meals and reducing food insecurity in our community.

Donation Guidelines

Donation Items

Non-perishable food items ready for distribution

Most Needed Items

  • Canned proteins (tuna, chicken, salmon)
  • Peanut butter and nut butters
  • Canned fruits and vegetables
  • Pasta, rice, and grains
  • Canned or dry beans
  • Cereal and oatmeal
  • Cooking oils
  • Shelf-stable milk
  • Baby food and formula
  • Hygiene products

Items We Cannot Accept

  • Expired food items
  • Homemade foods
  • Open or partially used items
  • Alcoholic beverages
  • Medications
  • Items requiring refrigeration (unless arranged)
  • Items with damaged packaging
  • Rusty or dented cans

Food Safety Guidelines

  • Check expiration dates before donating
  • Ensure packaging is intact and unopened
  • Store food in a cool, dry place before donating
  • Group similar items together when possible
  • Consider donating reusable bags with your food items

Organize a Food Drive

Step 1: Plan Your Food Drive

Choose a date, location, and goal for your food drive. Decide if you'll collect specific items or accept all non-perishable donations. Register your food drive with us to receive support materials and guidance.

We recommend running food drives for 1-2 weeks to maximize participation. Consider setting a specific goal, such as collecting 500 pounds of food or 300 items.

Step 2: Promote Your Food Drive

Spread the word about your food drive through social media, email, flyers, and word of mouth. We can provide digital promotional materials and collection bin signage to help you get started.

Be sure to clearly communicate the dates, locations, and most-needed items. Consider creating a theme or friendly competition to increase engagement.

Step 3: Collect Donations

Set up clearly marked collection bins in high-traffic areas. Check bins regularly and ensure they don't overflow. Keep track of donations received to measure progress toward your goal.

Consider having volunteers present during peak times to answer questions and thank donors. This personal touch can significantly increase participation.

Step 4: Deliver Your Donations

Once your food drive is complete, arrange to deliver the collected items to one of our distribution centers. For large collections, we may be able to arrange pickup. Be sure to call ahead to coordinate.

We'll provide you with a receipt and impact statement showing how many meals your donation will provide to those in need.

Frequently Asked Questions

What food items can I donate? +

We accept non-perishable food items such as canned goods (vegetables, fruits, meats, soups), pasta, rice, cereal, peanut butter, and other shelf-stable items. Please ensure all items are unopened and within their expiration date. See our Donation Guidelines section for a complete list of most-needed items.

Where can I drop off donations? +

We have multiple collection points throughout the city, including grocery stores, community centers, and our main warehouse. Check our Locations section for the drop-off point nearest to you. For large donations, we may be able to arrange a pickup service.

Can I donate money instead of food? +

Monetary donations are extremely valuable as they allow us to purchase fresh produce, dairy, and other perishable items that complement our non-perishable inventory. Every $1 donated helps provide up to 4 meals. You can donate securely online through our website or by mail.

How can I volunteer? +

We welcome volunteers for various roles including food sorting, distribution, driving, and administrative support. Visit our volunteer page to sign up and see available opportunities. Both regular and one-time volunteers are appreciated! We also offer group volunteering for companies, schools, and organizations.

Are my donations tax-deductible? +

Yes, we are a registered 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, so all donations (both food and monetary) are tax-deductible. We provide receipts for all donations upon request. For food donations, we can provide a receipt with an estimated value based on weight.
